5 Reasons Walking Is the Most Underrated Exercise

It doesn't require a gym, a subscription, or special gear. And the benefits go far beyond fitness.

In a world obsessed with high-intensity workouts and step-count competitions, walking often gets dismissed as "not real exercise." But research consistently shows that walking is one of the most powerful things you can do for your health — physical, mental, and social.

1. It boosts creativity

A Stanford study found that walking increases creative output by an average of 60%. Whether you're stuck on a problem at work or looking for inspiration, a walk can unlock ideas that sitting at a desk never will. There's a reason so many writers, composers, and entrepreneurs have been devoted walkers.

2. It reduces stress and anxiety

Walking in green spaces has been shown to lower cortisol levels within minutes. It activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the body's "rest and digest" mode — helping you feel calmer and more present. Even a 15-minute walk can make a measurable difference.

3. It's gentle on your body

Unlike running or HIIT, walking is low-impact. It strengthens your heart, improves circulation, and supports joint health without the injury risk. It's exercise you can do every single day, at any age, for the rest of your life.

4. It strengthens social bonds

Some of the best conversations happen side by side, not face to face. Walking with a friend, partner, or colleague removes the pressure of eye contact and creates a natural flow of dialogue. It's therapy without the therapist.

5. It helps you discover your own city

How well do you really know your neighbourhood? Walking forces you to slow down and notice things you'd miss in a car or on a bike — the street art on a side alley, the bakery that just opened, the park bench with the perfect view. Every walk is a chance to see something new.

The best workout is the one you actually do. And there's nothing easier to start than a walk.
